Joshua Tree Nationalpark

As the sun sets on our Joshua Tree adventure, Hidden Valley Campground emerges as a hidden gem, a tranquil refuge in the heart of this mesmerizing desert landscape. The vibrant hues painted across the sky, reflecting off the iconic rock formations, create a visual symphony that turns the campground into a haven of serenity. Setting up camp here offers not just shelter but a front-row seat to nature's nightly spectacle. For those seeking an adrenaline rush, the Mastodon Peak Trail starting from Cottonwood proves to be a thrilling odyssey. The trail's winding path through diverse terrains leads to a summit with panoramic views, rewarding hikers with a breathtaking vista of the rugged desert below. The effort expended along the trail pales in comparison to the awe-inspiring beauty that awaits at the peak. A leisurely stroll through the Cholla Cactus Garden is nothing short of surreal, especially during the golden hour. The spiky inhabitants of this garden come alive with a mesmerizing glow, creating an atmosphere that captivates the senses. This unique landscape, coupled with the serene ambiance of the desert, transforms the garden into a haven for photographers and nature enthusiasts alike. As we explore the local scene, the Joshua Tree Coffee Company stands out as a must-visit gem in town. Beyond just serving artisanal coffee, it offers a blend of caffeine and community vibes. Engage in conversation with fellow travelers, soak in the laid-back atmosphere, and let the essence of Joshua Tree permeate your senses. In every sip and every moment shared, the coffee shop becomes a part of the vibrant tapestry that makes Joshua Tree a destination worth savoring.

Death Valley Nationalpark

Furnace Creek Campground, with its captivating surroundings, provided the perfect base for our Death Valley escapade. From encounters with wildlife to the awe-inspiring beauty of Below Sea Level landmarks, our journey through Badwater Basin, Devils Golf Course, Artists Palette Drive, and Twenty Mule Team Canyon left us with memories etched in the unique canvas of Death Valley. As the sun set behind the desert horizon, we couldn't help but feel grateful for the opportunity to experience the extraordinary beauty of this desert oasis.

Alabama Hills

Camping in the Alabama Hills, with Tuttle Creek as our base, provided an immersive experience in nature's grandeur. From savoring pancake breakfasts at the Lone Pine Bakery to traversing the iconic Movie Road and marveling at the majestic presence of Mt. Whitney, every moment was a celebration of the unique wonders this desert landscape has to offer. The Alabama Hills stand as a testament to the beauty that awaits those who venture off the beaten path, making it a must-visit destination for any camping enthusiast. Our basecamp at Tuttle Creek Campground provided the perfect launchpad for exploring the Alabama Hills. The site offered a rustic charm and convenient proximity to Lone Pine, making it an ideal choice for nature enthusiasts and outdoor lovers. The starlit nights and crisp mountain air made our camping experience truly magical.

L.A. region

From the tranquil Kenney Grove Park to the coastal splendors of San Clemente, our road trip along the California coast is a celebration of nature, iconic landmarks, and culinary delights. By sidestepping the urban hustle until San Clemente, we've created a journey that captures the essence of California's coastal beauty. Whether basking in the sun on Malibu's beaches, strolling the Santa Monica Pier, exploring local Farmers Markets, or savoring the iconic In-N-Out Burger, every moment on this road trip is a reminder of the diverse and captivating experiences that define the Golden State.
